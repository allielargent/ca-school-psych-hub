# Disable or update browser extensions

## Managing Chrome Extensions

You can easily manage your Chrome extensions—including disabling, enabling, and updating them—through the **Extensions** page.

### 1. Accessing the Extensions Page

There are two main ways to quickly get to the management page:

* **Option A: Using the Menu**

  1. Click the **three-dot menu** (Customize and control Google Chrome) in the top right corner of your browser.
  2. Hover over **Extensions**.
  3. Click **Manage Extensions**.
* **Option B: Direct Link**

  1. Type or paste the following into your Chrome address bar: `chrome://extensions` and press **Enter**.

---

### 2. Disabling or Enabling Extensions

On the **Extensions** page, you'll see a card for every extension you have installed.

* To **Disable** an extension, click the **blue toggle switch** on the bottom right of the extension's card to turn it **off (gray)**. The extension will stop running until you turn it back on.
* To **Enable** an extension, click the **gray toggle switch** to turn it **on (blue)**.

---

### 3. Updating Extensions

Chrome is usually set to update extensions automatically. However, if you want to force an update immediately, follow these steps:

1. On the **Extensions** page, click the **Developer mode** toggle switch in the top right corner to turn it **on**. This will reveal new buttons.
2. Click the **Update** button (usually near the top left of the page).

Chrome will now check for and install any available updates for all your installed extensions.
