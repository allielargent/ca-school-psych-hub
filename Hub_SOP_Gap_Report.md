# Hub SOP Gap Report

Hub content referencing (or implying) a Parallel workflow without citing an official Help Center article, plus cases where the Help Center itself has no article to cite. Ordered by priority.

---

## HIGH priority

### 1. Report Writer is never named anywhere in the Hub
- **Hub location:** Report Writing Checklist (lines 793–802), Report Review Assistant (1344–1353), Assessment & Eligibility tab report-template reference (~1450s)
- **Missing SOP:** The actual Parallel tool providers use to write reports — 14 official articles exist: [Report Writer Homepage and Creating a Report](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/33751250337819-Report-Writer-Homepage-and-Creating-a-Report), [Overview of Templates](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/33751353805723-Report-Writer-Overview-of-Templates), [Eligibility Criteria (Psych Reports Only)](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/33751509827739-Report-Writer-Eligibility-Criteria-Psych-Reports-Only), [Submitting & Reviewing Your Report](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/33754006843291-Report-Writer-Submitting-Reviewing-Your-Report), and 10 others.
- **Why it matters:** The Hub tells providers *what* a legally defensible report needs but never tells them *where* they actually write it or how the tool's own eligibility-criteria feature works. A new CA school psychologist reading only this Hub would not know Report Writer exists.
- **Recommended next action:** Add a "Related Parallel Help Center Articles" block to the Report Writing Checklist and Report Review Assistant pointing to the Report Writer Overview series; add one incorporated sentence noting that eligibility language generated by the Hub's own tool should be entered via Report Writer's own Eligibility Criteria feature.
- **Priority:** High

### 2. No California entry in the Help Center's own State-Specific Guides library
- **Hub location:** State Resources tab (1504–1528) — this entire Hub exists to be the California guide
- **Missing SOP:** A "California Special Education Guide" analogous to the 13 that exist for AZ, GA, IL, IN, KY, ME, MI, MO, NY, OR, PA, TX, WA in Help Center category *School Psychologist Providers > State Specific Guides and Resources*.
- **Why it matters:** This is the most direct content gap found. Every other state Parallel serves has an official Help Center guide except the one this Hub is built for. It suggests the Hub itself may be the *unofficial* stand-in for what should eventually be an official Parallel CA guide — worth surfacing to whoever owns Help Center content.
- **Recommended next action:** Flag to the Help Center/Knowledge content owner that a California guide is missing from that library; in the meantime, do not imply on the Hub that Parallel has published an official CA guide beyond this Hub itself.
- **Priority:** High

### 3. Billing guidance is entirely absent from the Hub
- **Hub location:** No section discusses billing at all (confirmed: zero occurrences of "billing" in the file)
- **Missing SOP:** [Hourly Billing (not included in flat rate assessments)](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/36999884708507--School-Psychologists-Hourly-Billing-not-included-in-flat-rate-assessments), [Functional Behavioral Assessments (FBA)](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/46488076348827-Functional-Behavioral-Assessments-FBA) (billing-scope section), [Evaluation Results Meeting & Documentation](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/36966336721563-Evaluation-Results-Meeting-Documentation) (meeting-time billing rule)
- **Why it matters:** Providers doing FBA work, extended meetings, or evaluation activity beyond the flat-rate scope need to know what's billed hourly vs. included — getting this wrong is a real compensation/compliance issue, not just a knowledge gap.
- **Recommended next action:** Add a short "Billing & Scope" note to the FBA/BIP Quick Reference and to the Meeting Preparation Guide, each linking to its matching Help Center article. Do not restate specific dollar/hour figures in the Hub — link only, since billing policy can change.
- **Priority:** High

### 4. FBA billing/scope workflow not connected to the Hub's clinical FBA/BIP guidance
- **Hub location:** FBA Quick Reference (729–743), BIP Quick Reference (745–755), FBA→BIP Workflow (1110–1120), FBA/BIP Assistant (1304–1313)
- **Missing SOP:** [Functional Behavioral Assessments (FBA)](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/46488076348827-Functional-Behavioral-Assessments-FBA) — explicitly states BIP development/monitoring is billed hourly and is not a standalone service line.
- **Why it matters:** A provider could reasonably assume, from the Hub alone, that FBA/BIP work is simply part of "the evaluation," with no visibility into Parallel's specific billing treatment. This has direct compensation impact.
- **Recommended next action:** Add a short source note under the FBA/BIP Quick References: "Source: Parallel Provider Portal Help Center — FBA billing and scope, last updated 2026-05-13" with a link.
- **Priority:** High

### 5. District onboarding: "District 101" (real, live Pathway feature) vs. the Hub's empty District Hub placeholders
- **Hub location:** District Hub tab (1529–1550) — only `[District A]`/`[District B]` placeholder cards exist; no real content populated
- **Missing SOP:** [What is District 101?](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/43762478641563-What-is-District-101) — Parallel's actual live source for school-specific contacts, blackout dates, service dates, and local procedures, replacing the old Provider Handbook.
- **Why it matters:** Right now, a provider looking for district-specific info in the Hub finds only placeholders and no pointer to the real, currently-populated Pathway feature that already has this data for their actual assignment.
- **Recommended next action:** Add a prominent link/callout at the top of the District Hub tab pointing to District 101 in Pathway as the authoritative live source, while keeping the Hub's own template as a separate, deeper clinical-procedure layer to fill in over time. See Conflict Report for why these two should not be presented as the same thing.
- **Priority:** High

### 6. WJ-IV, Riverside, and named cognitive/achievement instruments never mentioned
- **Hub location:** Assessment Planning Guide/Planner (~1440s, 1914–1920)
- **Missing SOP:** WISC-V, WAIS-5, WAIS-IV, WIAT-4, WJ-IV (Cognitive Abilities, Tests of Achievement, Tests of Oral Language), DIVA-5 (Adult/Young), MIGDAS-2 — 11 articles in *School Psychologist Assessment Library*; scoring platform guidance in [Scoring with Riverside Score](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/23628599958939-Scoring-with-Riverside-Score) and [Scoring with Q-Global](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/23628637383323-Scoring-with-Q-Global).

A tool-agnostic Assessment Planner without any link to Parallel's actual approved instrument library and scoring platforms leaves a real operational gap.
- **Recommended next action:** Add a compact "Parallel Assessment Library & Scoring Tools" links block (top 5–7 instruments/tools) to the Assessment Planning Guide and Clinical Toolbox's Assessment Planner.
- **Priority:** High

### 7. Mandated reporter guidance is generic; Parallel's actual CA-specific training requirements are richer and not linked
- **Hub location:** FAQ 1258 ("Am I a mandated reporter when working remotely?"), Crisis & Safety Checklist (1041), Start Here onboarding item (1422)
- **Missing SOP:** [California Mandated Reporter Training Requirements for School Personnel](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/51543899095067-California-Mandated-Reporter-Training-Requirements-for-School-Personnel) (AB 1432 + AB 1913/SB 848), [California AB 1172 Positive Behavior Training Requirements](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/51585450834203-California-AB-1172-Positive-Behavior-Training-Requirements), [Parallel Policy on Detecting & Reporting Domestic Child Abuse/Mandatory Reporting](https://providerhelp.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/21630622397083-Parallel-Policy-on-Detecting-Reporting-Domestic-Child-Abuse-Mandatory-Reporting)
