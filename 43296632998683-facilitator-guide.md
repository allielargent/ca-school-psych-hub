# Facilitator Guide

The facilitator plays a crucial role in ensuring the success of the students in teletherapy by serving as the link between the student, provider, teacher and school.

#### Roles and Responsibilities:

* Inform the provider of absences or missed sessions as soon as possible.
* Serve as the liaison between the teacher and provider for scheduling.
* Share student absences or upcoming school activities.

#### Before the Session:

* Check your email for the day’s schedule
* Communicate any student absences or changes in student availability to the Provider.
* Set up a private and quiet session area.
* Ensure the computer is ready for sessions: equipment is in place, plugged in and working.
* Walk the student(s) to the session.

#### During the Session:

* Sign the student(s) into the session via the unique Parallel link provided to you.
* If confidentiality is needed for the session - be nearby (within eyesight, but not earshot) to assist with student needs.
* Minimize any distracting sounds or disruptors.
* Collaborate with the Provider on specific student needs.
* Remain present during the entire session.

After the Session:

* Walk the student(s) from the session.

Best practices:

* Exchange phone numbers with the Provider.
* Make sure students do not leave the therapy room tab/window on their device.

Troubleshooting:

* Log out of your computer and then log back in.
* Refresh your page.
