# Example TEST Article: Using Co-Browsing in Your Pathway Session

**Note:** This article was last edited via API on May 7, 2026.

Co-browsing is a built-in Pathway feature that lets you open a website inside your session window so you and your student can view it together. Unlike Screen Share, co-browsing can give your student the ability to click, type, and navigate the site directly — making it useful for interactive activities like browser-based games, online forms, or reading exercises.

This article walks you through everything you need to know: what co-browsing is, how to start it, how to give your student control, how audio works (a common source of confusion), and what to do if something is not loading correctly. It also covers when Screen Share is the better choice and how to switch between the two tools during a session.

If you are already comfortable with the basics and just need help with a specific issue, you can jump directly to [Troubleshooting](#h_01KQW23HPRH289P115Y369RDWW) or [Website Compatibility](#h_01KQW23HPRQF8GAP8G7XMWD2JH).

## What Is Co-Browsing?

Co-browsing lets you open a website inside your Pathway session so you and your student can view it together — and, when you choose, let your student click and interact with it directly.

It is different from Screen Share in a few important ways:

|  | Co-Browsing | Screen Share |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Student can interact | Yes, if you give them control | No — view only |
| Student hears website audio | No | Yes |
| Only the website is visible | Yes | No — your full screen is visible |
| Works on student iPad | No | Yes |

Use co-browsing when your student needs to click, type, or navigate a site. Use Screen Share when audio from the site is important, or when co-browsing is not working.

**Before you start:** Co-browsing requires a desktop or laptop computer running **Google Chrome**. It does not work on iPads, Kindles, or other tablets. If you are using Safari, switch to Chrome before your session.

## Starting a Co-Browsing Session

1. From inside your active Pathway session, click the co-browsing icon in the toolbar.  
   *[SCREENSHOT: toolbar with co-browsing icon highlighted and labeled]*
2. A panel will open. Type or paste the website address you want to visit and press Enter.
3. The website will load for both you and your student.

**If the site does not load or shows an error:** Some websites block co-browsing on their end — this is not something you can change. See the Website Compatibility section below for more detail.

*[SCREENSHOT: co-browsing panel open with a website loaded]*

## Switching Between Co-Browsing and the Whiteboard

Co-browsing and the whiteboard are both available during your session at the same time. You can switch between them freely — your co-browsing session stays open in the background when you switch to the whiteboard, and vice versa. You will not lose your place on the website.

## Giving Your Student Control

By default, your student can see the website but cannot click or type on it. There are three ways to give them control, and **they do not all work the same way.** This is one of the most common points of confusion.

### Option 1 — Settings Panel Toggle (Recommended for Back-and-Forth Sessions)

1. While co-browsing is active, open the settings panel on the left side of the screen.  
   *[SCREENSHOT: left panel open with the student control toggle visible]*
2. Turn on the student control setting.
3. Both you and your student can now interact with the page at the same time.
4. To take control back, return to this panel and turn the setting off.

### Option 2 — Edit Pencil Icon on Your Student's Video

1. Look for the small edit pencil icon on your student's video feed.  
   *[SCREENSHOT: student video tile with the pencil icon highlighted]*
2. Click it to give your student control.

### Option 3 — Arrow Button Inside the Co-Browsing Window

1. Inside the co-browsing window, click the arrow icon from the options visible on screen.  
   *[SCREENSHOT: the icons inside the co-browsing window, with the arrow icon labeled]*
2. This transfers control to your student.

**Important:** Options 2 and 3 transfer control to your student entirely — meaning you will lose your own ability to interact with the page until you take it back. If you use Option 3 (the arrow button inside co-browsing), you will need to open the left panel each time you want to regain control.  
  
If you are doing an activity where you need to switch control back and forth frequently — like taking turns in a game — **use the settings panel toggle (Option 1)** to keep shared access active throughout.

**If you have more than one student in the session:** Only one student can have control at a time. Giving control to one student does not affect the others.

**When no student has control:** Students cannot click, type, or draw on the co-browsing page — even if they have drawing access elsewhere in the session. This is by design.

## Audio: What Your Student Can and Cannot Hear

This is the most commonly misunderstood part of co-browsing.

Your student can hear **you** through your microphone. They **cannot** hear sounds coming from the website itself — for example, audio from UltimateSLP, YouTube, or other activity sites. Co-browsing only shares the visual content of the page, not its audio.

**Using UltimateSLP or another site that plays audio as part of the activity?** Your student will not hear it during co-browsing. If the audio is essential, use Screen Share for that activity instead.  
  
Keep in mind: Screen Share does not let your student interact with the screen. You may need to switch tools depending on what the activity requires.

## Website Compatibility

Co-browsing works with most general websites, but some sites intentionally block it. If a site does not load inside co-browsing, or if you see a security or access error:

* The website itself has blocked the type of proxy that co-browsing uses.
* There is nothing you or Parallel can do to override it.
* Known examples: certain assessment platforms that require a login (such as Riverside Insights), and sites that detect and block third-party access.

If you run into this, use Screen Share as a backup for that activity.

## Troubleshooting

If co-browsing is not loading, your student cannot see what you see, or the session is freezing, try these steps in order:

1. **Refresh your browser.** Close and reopen the co-browsing panel. This resolves most loading issues.
2. **Close extra tabs and applications.** Co-browsing uses more bandwidth than a regular video call. Closing anything you do not need frees up resources.
3. **Confirm you are using Google Chrome.** Safari and other browsers do not work reliably with co-browsing.
4. **Clear your browser cache.** In Chrome, click the three-dot menu in the top right corner, then go to **Settings > Privacy and Security > Clear browsing data**. Select **Cached images and files**, then click **Clear data**. Restart Chrome when done.
5. **Run the Parallel connection test.** Go to [telehealth.parallellearning.com/connection-test](https://telehealth.parallellearning.com/connection-test) and look at the **Co-Browsing** result. If that specific test fails while others pass, the issue is likely with your network or a firewall.

**If your student is on a school-managed device and co-browsing consistently fails for them:** Their school's network may be blocking the service that powers co-browsing. The school's IT team would need to allow access to `*.surfly.com`. You can refer them to the [Parallel Firewall Configuration and Testing Guide](https://help.parallellearning.com/hc/en-us/articles/20457820922011-Parallel-Firewall-Configuration-and-Testing-Guide).

**If none of these steps help:** Switch to Screen Share for the remainder of the session. Submit a support ticket with your session details — including the date, time, and what you observed — so our team can investigate.

## When to Use Screen Share Instead

Switch to Screen Share when:

* Your student needs to hear audio from a website
* The website you need is not working in co-browsing
* Your student is on an iPad or tablet
* Co-browsing is consistently failing during a session

**Note:** Switching to Screen Share during a session does not end your co-browsing session — you can return to it at any time.
